Horatius Cocles

Legendary Defender of the Pons Sublicius Bridge

Publius Horatius Cocles was an officer in the army of the early Roman Republic who famously defended the Pons Sublicius from the invading army of Etruscan King Lars Porsena of Clusium in the late 6th century BC.

He was a nephew of the consul, Marcus Horatius Pulvillus, and is said to have obtained his agnomen (nickname), Cocles, meaning "one-eyed", because he lost an eye in the Battle of the Sublician Bridge.

Horatius was a junior guard on the bridge when a sudden assualt by King Lars' men began. Horatius and two other defenders stood their ground on the bridge despite being ordered to retreat, using the bodies of fallen enemies as part of their shield wall. Horatius commanded the men to destroy the bridge behind him, fighting off enemies and suffering multiple injuries until the bridge was destroying, thwarting the Etruscan army's advance. Once the bridge was destroyed, he leaped into the river swimming across to escape with his life.
